Taking the Branch Out of Banking

How a Fortune 500 bank replaced a multi-person, in-branch process with an end-to-end digital experience that earned recognition in Barlows Research's Game Changers publication.

The Challenge

There was a banking task that required something almost no modern product asks of its users anymore: showing up in person. Two account holders had to physically travel to a brick-and-mortar branch location together, present photo identification, and work their way through a significant stack of paperwork before the process could be completed. It was time-consuming, logistically inconvenient, and increasingly out of step with how customers expected to interact with their bank.

The challenge was that the process was necessary and had meaningful legal and compliance impacts. Identity verification, consent, authorization. Without the paper trail that the branch had always relied on. And doing it in a way that felt simple and intuitive to users who might not be particularly tech-savvy.

A process that once required multiple people to physically travel to a branch location and sign a stack of paperwork was replaced by two emails and a link.

The Approach

We did a lot of due diligence and had a lot of conversations with internal risk partners. We did this for months, before a designer ever created a pixel or an engineer wrote any code. After a long series of meetings, we had identified all the areas of risk we needed to be concerned with and all of our proposed solutions and workarounds to remediate them. We were finally ready to design an end-to-end digital enrollment flow built around an invitation model. Person A initiates the process from within their existing online account and sends an invitation to Person B. Person B receives a personalized email with a secure link that takes them directly into a guided enrollment flow. No branch visit. No printer. No ink.

The enrollment flow itself was built to handle the complexity invisibly. Every compliance checkpoint, every verification step, every consent acknowledgment was woven into a clean, step-by-step experience that walked the user through exactly what was needed and nothing more. We worked closely with legal and compliance teams throughout, treating their requirements not as obstacles but as design inputs.

Getting the invitation mechanic right was particularly important. The email had to be clear enough that someone unfamiliar with the process would immediately understand and feel confident clicking the link. We tested multiple versions, refined the language, and paid careful attention to the moments where users were most likely to drop off or fail.

The Outcome

It took over two years from the first meeting that was held to product launch. But when it did go live, we were treated to an 82% adoption rate among target users. This was an astronomical success in any industry, but especially in banking. Typically, you should be happy with adoption over 10% for anything you build in that space. Once customers discovered this experience, they flocked to it. Operating expenses dropped by 13% as branch-assisted completions gave way to self-serve digital ones. The product eliminated ink signatures entirely from the process.

The work was recognized externally as well. Barlow Research featured the end-to-end experience in their Game Changers publication, which highlights standout innovation in financial services. It was a meaningfully different kind of experience.

More than the numbers or the recognition, this was a reminder of what can happen when you solve complex problems that add value for customers. This was a compliance-heavy, operationally complex problem, which few Product Managers want to solve. Everyone wants to chase the technical ones.
